16-bit pixel art dungeon tileset moodboard

A visual reference board for creating an indoor dungeon tileset for a Zelda-like top-down 2D action RPG.

Mood/Theme:
- Classic Zelda dungeon aesthetic
- Dark, mysterious, slightly dangerous
- Ancient temple/ruin atmosphere
- Room-based rectangular design

Color Palette:
- Stone grays (walls, floor, pillars)
- Dark browns (wood elements, doors)
- Moss greens (aged, overgrown accents)
- Torch orange/yellow (lighting accents)
- Black (pits, shadows, voids)
- Accent colors per dungeon theme

Required Tiles (16x16 each):

Floor Tiles:
- Stone floor (base, cracked variants)
- Decorative floor patterns
- Pit/void (black, deadly)
- Spike trap (up and down states)
- Pressure plate (pressed/released)

Wall Tiles:
- Solid wall (top/bottom/sides)
- Wall corners (inner/outer)
- Bombable wall (cracked variant)
- Wall torch holder

Doors:
- Open door passage
- Closed door (normal)
- Locked door (keyhole visible)
- Boss door (ornate, large)
- Shutter door (combat lock)

Interactive Objects:
- Treasure chest (open/closed)
- Switch/lever (on/off)
- Push block (moveable stone)
- Pot/vase (breakable)
- Statue/decoration

Hazards:
- Spike floor
- Fire trap
- Arrow trap hole (wall)
- Moving platform edge

Decorative:
- Pillars
- Skull decor
- Chains
- Cobwebs
- Moss patches

Lighting Considerations:
- Torches provide warm pools of light
- Corners are darker
- Slight vignette feel in rooms

Style Notes:
- Primarily grayscale with accent colors
- Room edges clearly defined
- Grid-friendly design
- All tiles 16x16 base

Reference Games:
- A Link to the Past dungeons
- Link's Awakening dungeons
- Binding of Isaac room layouts

Output:
- Moodboard showing dungeon atmosphere
- Sample room layout example
- Key interactive tiles highlighted
- Color palette strip
